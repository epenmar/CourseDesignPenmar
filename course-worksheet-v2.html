Course Level Objectives

Define what students will do and learn — start with Expected Learning Outcomes (ELOs), then derive Course Level Objectives (CLOs).

💡 Tips for writing objectives: Start each objective with one measurable action verb. Avoid vague verbs like "know," "understand," or "learn." A strong objective looks like: "Students will be able to [verb] [specific knowledge/skill] by [method or context]."
📐 Action Verbs & Cognitive Levels Expand for a guide to choosing the right verbs
▾

Learning objectives are organized into six levels of cognitive complexity — from recalling facts up to creating original work. Use this to pick the right verb for the level of thinking you're asking students to do. CLOs (Course Level Objectives) typically target lower-to-mid levels (understand, apply). ELOs (Expected Learning Outcomes) should use higher levels (apply, analyze, evaluate, create). MLOs (Module Learning Outcomes) can span any level depending on the module.

1. Remember — Recall facts & basic concepts
Verbs: define, list, memorize, recall, repeat, state, identify, name, recognize
2. Understand — Explain ideas or concepts
Verbs: classify, describe, discuss, explain, locate, recognize, report, select, translate, paraphrase, summarize
3. Apply — Use information in new situations
Verbs: execute, implement, solve, use, demonstrate, interpret, operate, illustrate, practice, compute
4. Analyze — Draw connections among ideas
Verbs: compare, contrast, differentiate, distinguish, categorize, deconstruct, examine, organize, relate, structure
5. Evaluate — Justify a stand or decision
Verbs: appraise, argue, assess, critique, defend, judge, justify, rank, recommend, support, validate
6. Create — Produce new or original work
Verbs: design, develop, formulate, construct, compose, generate, hypothesize, invent, plan, produce, synthesize

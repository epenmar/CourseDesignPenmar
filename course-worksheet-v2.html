Course Learning Outcomes

Define what students will learn — Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs) and Expected Learning Outcomes (ELOs).

💡 Tips for writing objectives: Start each objective with one measurable action verb. Avoid vague verbs like "know," "understand," or "learn." A strong objective looks like: "Students will be able to [verb] [specific knowledge/skill] by [method or context]."
📐 Action Verbs & Cognitive Levels Expand for a guide to choosing the right verbs
▾

Learning objectives are organized into six levels of cognitive complexity — from recalling facts up to creating original work. Use this to pick the right verb for the level of thinking you're asking students to do. CLOs (Course Learning Outcomes) typically target lower-to-mid levels (understand, apply). ELOs (Expected Learning Outcomes) should use higher levels (apply, analyze, evaluate, create). MLOs (Module Learning Outcomes) can span any level depending on the module.

1. Remember — Recall facts & basic concepts
Verbs: define, list, memorize, recall, repeat, state, identify, name, recognize
2. Understand — Explain ideas or concepts
Verbs: classify, describe, discuss, explain, locate, recognize, report, select, translate, paraphrase, summarize
3. Apply — Use information in new situations
Verbs: execute, implement, solve, use, demonstrate, interpret, operate, illustrate, practice, compute
4. Analyze — Draw connections among ideas
Verbs: compare, contrast, differentiate, distinguish, categorize, deconstruct, examine, organize, relate, structure
5. Evaluate — Justify a stand or decision
Verbs: appraise, argue, assess, critique, defend, judge, justify, rank, recommend, support, validate
6. Create — Produce new or original work
Verbs: design, develop, formulate, construct, compose, generate, hypothesize, invent, plan, produce, synthesize
✨ Want personalized feedback? Open the AI Design Assistant to check your objectives against these cognitive levels, get verb suggestions, or understand how CLOs, ELOs, and MLOs relate.

MNS 521 — AI Technologies • Summer 2026 Session B
ASU online

Instructor: Dariush Navabi (dnavabi@asu.edu)

Instructional Designer: Elisa Penmar

Credits: 3 • Prerequisites: None

Course Learning Outcomes

CLOs — Students will learn to:

1. Understand the evolution and core differences between major AI paradigms

2. Identify appropriate abstractions and modeling techniques for AI solutions

3. Recognize the mechanics of machine learning algorithms

4. Comprehend structural requirements for integrated AI architectures

5. Appreciate ethical complexities including fairness and transparency in AI

6. Interpret complex AI system trade-offs for stakeholder communication

ELOs — Students will be able to:

1. Differentiate between major AI paradigms including search, knowledge representation, and probabilistic reasoning

2. Formulate real-world problems using AI abstractions such as agents, state spaces, and constraints

3. Describe core machine learning approaches and evaluate their performance

4. Analyze and design high-level AI system architectures

5. Critically evaluate AI technologies for fairness, transparency, and societal impact

6. Communicate AI system designs and tradeoffs to technical and non-technical audiences
